Journey to Gaya: A Soul-Stirring Pilgrimage in Bihar

By Rahul Verma | Travel Correspondent June 14, 2026

Echoes of Enlightenment: A Journey Through the Sacred Dualities of Gaya

There are few places on earth where the material world thins out so completely, leaving you face-to-face with the absolute weight of human faith. Gaya is one of those rare coordinates. Nestled along the banks of the mystical Falgu River and cradled by rugged, protective hills, this ancient city functions as a twin portal to salvation.

Things to Do in Gaya

  • Heritage walks: Explore the old city with guided walks visiting key historic and religious sites.
  • Monastery visits: Gaya is home to numerous international monasteries, reflecting diverse Buddhist traditions.
  • Pay Respects at Vishnupad Temple: See the 40-cm footprint of Lord Vishnu incised into solid basalt rock and appreciate the grand granite architecture built by Queen Ahilya Bai Holkar.
  • Marvel at the Great Buddha Statue:Stand in awe before the majestic, serene 80-foot stone statue of Buddha in a meditative pose.
  • Explore International Monasteries: Tour the stunning, culturally distinct Thai, Royal Bhutan, Japanese, and Tibetan monasteries scattered around Bodh Gaya.
  • Taste Local Sweets: Treat yourself to Gaya's famous traditional sweets, Tilkut (pounded sesame and jaggery) and Anarsa.

The Mahabodhi Temple is a globally revered Buddhist site, where monks are pictured meditating near the descendants of the original Bodhi Tree.

  • Mahabodhi Temple and Complex:A UNESCO World Heritage site, the main temple and its extensive complex of stupas and shrines center around the revered Bodhi Tree, where the Buddha achieved enlightenment.The Mahabodhi Temple Complex in Bodh Gaya (located in the Gaya district of Bihar) is one of the most revered sacred sites in the world. It marks the exact location where Siddhartha Gautama attained enlightenment under the Bodhi Tree around 528 BCE, becoming the Buddha.
    The first temple at the site was constructed by the Mauryan Emperor Ashoka the Great in the 3rd century BCE. He erected a shrine and a protective railing around the sacred tree. However, the grand brick structure standing today dates back largely to the late Gupta Empire period (around the 5th or 6th century CE), making it one of the oldest brick structures surviving in eastern India.
  • Great Buddha Statue: This colossal, serene 80-foot stone statue is a prominent landmark, depicting the Buddha in meditation. The complex offers a peaceful setting for reflection.The Great Buddha Statue (often called the 80-Foot Buddha) is one of the most iconic and visually striking landmarks in Bodh Gaya. Located just a short walk from the Mahabodhi Temple complex, it stands as a towering symbol of peace, drawing thousands of pilgrims and tourists every day.
    Unveiled on November 18, 1989, by the 14th Dalai Lama, the statue was built with the support of the Daijokyo Buddhist Sect of Japan, creating a beautiful cross-cultural link between Indian heritage and Japanese Buddhist tradition.The statue stands an impressive 25 meters (82 feet) tall, including the pedestal. It depicts the Buddha in the Dhyana Mudra (meditation posture), sitting cross-legged on a giant lotus flower.
  • Vishnupad Temple: Located on the banks of the Falgu River, this temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and features a 40cm footprint, considered sacred by Hindu pilgrims who perform final rites here.The foundation of the temple is tied to a powerful legend from the Gaya Mahatmya texts regarding a demon king named Gayasur.
    Gayasur performed intense penance and received a boon that made him so pure that anyone who looked at or touched him would instantly attain salvation (moksha). This disrupted the cosmic balance of karma, rendering the concepts of righteous living and punishment redundant.

    To restore order, Lord Vishnu devised a plan to pin Gayasur down. Vishnu pressed his right foot heavily onto the demon's chest, subduing him permanently into the earth. Transformed into the rocky hills that form the backdrop of Gaya today, Gayasur made a final request: that this land be eternally blessed, and anyone who offered food (Pind Daan) to their ancestors here would grant those departed souls immediate liberation. Lord Vishnu granted the boon, leaving his divine footprint behind as a permanent seal of the promise.
  • Gaya Ghats:The riverfront area, including the historic Vishnupad Temple, is lined with ghats where pilgrims gather for religious ceremonies, offerings, and ritual baths.
